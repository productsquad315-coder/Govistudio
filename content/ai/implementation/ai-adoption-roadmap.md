# AI Adoption Roadmap

An AI adoption roadmap is a structured plan that guides a business through the phased implementation of AI systems, from initial assessment through full organizational deployment and continuous optimization.

## How It Works
Input: Business objectives, process priorities, technology landscape, and resource constraints
Processing: AI adoption roadmaps define phases, milestones, success metrics, and dependencies for systematic AI deployment
Output: A phased AI deployment plan with defined priorities, timelines, integration requirements, and measurable outcomes

## Use Cases
- Creating a three-phase AI adoption plan starting with sales automation, then operations, then finance
- Defining success metrics for each AI deployment phase to validate ROI before expansion
- Mapping AI integration requirements against existing technology infrastructure
- Building a change management plan to support employee adoption of AI workflows
- Establishing an AI governance framework alongside the technical adoption roadmap

## Benefits
- Provides a clear, sequenced path for AI adoption without overwhelming the organization
- Ensures highest-impact areas are prioritized for early deployment
- Aligns AI investment with business strategy and measurable outcomes
- Reduces implementation risk through phased validation and controlled rollouts
- Creates organizational alignment and stakeholder commitment to AI adoption

## FAQ
### How long does a typical AI adoption roadmap span?
Roadmaps typically cover twelve to twenty-four months, with phased milestones and ongoing optimization cycles.


### Who should be involved in creating an AI adoption roadmap?
Business leadership, operations management, IT, and department heads who own the processes being automated.


### Should AI adoption start with a pilot?
Yes. Beginning with a focused pilot in a high-impact area validates results and builds organizational confidence before broader rollout.


### How do businesses prioritize which AI systems to implement first?
Prioritize by impact (time saved, cost reduced, revenue generated) and feasibility (data availability, integration complexity, process clarity).


### Can an AI adoption roadmap be adjusted after it begins?
Yes. Roadmaps should be reviewed regularly and adjusted based on results, business priorities, and new opportunities.
