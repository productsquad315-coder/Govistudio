# AI Strategy for Businesses

An AI strategy for traditional businesses is a defined plan for how AI systems will be deployed, prioritized, and scaled to achieve specific business objectives including cost reduction, revenue growth, operational efficiency, and competitive positioning.

## How It Works
Input: Business objectives, operational challenges, technology landscape, competitive environment, and investment capacity
Processing: Strategy development identifies AI opportunities, prioritizes investments, defines success metrics, and creates a phased deployment roadmap
Output: A documented AI strategy with prioritized investments, phased deployment plans, ROI targets, and governance frameworks

## Use Cases
- Defining a three-year AI strategy that prioritizes sales automation, then operations, then finance
- Creating an AI investment roadmap aligned with annual business planning cycles
- Developing an AI competitive strategy to differentiate from competitors still using manual processes
- Building an AI data strategy to ensure data quality and accessibility for planned AI deployments
- Designing an AI governance framework as part of a comprehensive AI strategy

## Benefits
- Ensures AI investments are aligned with business objectives rather than technology trends
- Prioritizes highest-impact opportunities for early ROI delivery
- Creates organizational alignment around a shared AI vision and plan
- Manages AI investment risk through phased, validated deployment
- Builds a compounding strategic advantage as AI capabilities grow over time

## FAQ
### Who should be involved in creating a business AI strategy?
Senior leadership, operations management, finance, IT, and department heads who own high-impact processes.


### How does AI strategy differ from IT strategy?
AI strategy focuses on intelligence, automation, and business outcome delivery. IT strategy focuses on technology infrastructure and operations.


### How often should a business AI strategy be reviewed?
AI strategy should be reviewed annually and updated as business priorities, results, and technology capabilities evolve.


### Can small businesses have an AI strategy?
Yes. A simple, focused AI strategy that prioritizes one or two high-impact automation opportunities is appropriate for businesses of any size.
