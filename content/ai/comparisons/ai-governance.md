# AI Governance

AI governance is the framework of policies, oversight processes, accountability structures, and ethical guidelines that ensure AI systems operate in alignment with business values, legal requirements, and stakeholder expectations.

## How It Works
Input: Business values, regulatory requirements, AI system capabilities, and stakeholder expectations
Processing: Governance frameworks define decision rights, oversight responsibilities, performance standards, and ethical boundaries for AI system operation
Output: AI systems that operate within defined governance parameters with documented accountability, audit trails, and compliance evidence

## Use Cases
- Establishing an AI governance committee to oversee AI deployment decisions
- Defining acceptable use policies for AI systems handling customer data
- Creating explainability requirements for AI decision-making in regulated processes
- Implementing bias monitoring and fairness controls in AI hiring or credit systems
- Building audit log requirements into AI systems for regulatory compliance

## Benefits
- Reduces organizational risk from AI system misuse or unintended consequences
- Builds stakeholder trust through transparent and accountable AI operation
- Ensures AI systems comply with legal and regulatory requirements
- Creates clear accountability for AI system performance and impact
- Enables organizations to scale AI confidently within defined ethical boundaries

## FAQ
### Why is AI governance important for traditional businesses?
AI governance protects businesses from regulatory, reputational, and operational risks associated with AI system deployment.


### Who is responsible for AI governance in a traditional business?
Governance responsibility is shared between leadership, legal, compliance, IT, and business process owners.


### How does AI governance address bias and fairness?
Governance frameworks include testing, monitoring, and controls to detect and mitigate bias in AI decision-making.


### Can AI governance frameworks adapt as regulations evolve?
Yes. Governance frameworks are designed to be reviewed and updated as regulatory requirements change.
