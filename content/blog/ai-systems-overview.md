# AI Systems Overview

This overview covers what AI systems are, how they are structured, where they deliver value, and how traditional businesses can approach them practically.

WHAT AI SYSTEMS ARE

AI systems are software-based platforms that combine artificial intelligence with workflow automation to automate decisions, processes, and tasks in business operations. Unlike standalone AI features or generic software tools, AI systems are end-to-end operational platforms — they receive inputs, apply intelligence, take actions, and deliver outcomes within defined business workflows.

HOW AI SYSTEMS ARE STRUCTURED

Every AI system has four core components. The input layer receives data from business sources — emails, forms, CRM records, documents, and system events. The intelligence layer applies AI models to analyze inputs, make decisions, and determine actions. The workflow layer orchestrates the sequence of actions that follow each decision. The output layer delivers results — updated records, sent communications, generated reports, or triggered alerts — to connected systems and stakeholders.

This four-layer structure allows AI systems to handle complex, multi-step business processes reliably and at scale.

THE MAIN CATEGORIES OF AI SYSTEMS FOR BUSINESS

Process automation systems handle defined workflows end-to-end — onboarding, invoice processing, lead management, and reporting are examples.

Decision support systems evaluate inputs and produce decisions or recommendations — lead scoring, credit decisioning, and escalation routing fall into this category.

Intelligence and analytics systems analyze business data to generate insights and predictions — forecasting, performance analytics, and anomaly detection are examples.

Agent systems take autonomous action across multiple tools and workflows — AI sales assistants, AI support agents, and AI operations monitors are examples.

HOW AI SYSTEMS CONNECT TO EXISTING BUSINESSES

AI systems integrate with existing business tools through APIs and direct connectors. They do not require businesses to replace their CRM, ERP, or other operational platforms. They add intelligence and automation to the technology already in place.

## FAQ
### What is the difference between an AI system and an AI application?
An AI application is a specific tool. An AI system is an integrated operational platform that combines multiple AI capabilities into unified business workflows.


### Can AI systems be built for any industry?
Yes. AI systems are built for specific industries and workflows, with customization to fit the unique requirements of each business.
